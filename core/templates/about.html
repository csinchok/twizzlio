About Twizzlio

People find conflict entertaining. It's why we have boxing matches and the Oscars.

This love of conflict extends to social networking. Facebook and Twitter, in addition to be useful, are also a popularity contest. Why do people need to know how many friends you have? Or how many followers you have? How popular you are doesn't change what you're saying.

It serves as a game. When you post to Twitter, in addition to delivering information, you're also trying to gain followers. To earn more points and raise a score measured in people. And this isn't a bad thing. It's a fun side game and a normal part of human interaction.

What we've done with Twizzlio is gamify the already existing game of social networking. You pick the friends, celebrities, and brands you have online ties with and predict will gain popularity. Then you battle another player by setting your picks against theirs. After one week Twizzlio compares the gain in online popularity by each team, and declares a winner.

Twizzlio comes with built in monetization. Companies and celebrities have the option of rewarding players that trusted them in their team. If, and only if, they win, players are presented with special offers from the brands they used. And they can share these deals with the friend that was in their team. This allows companies to build ties with their biggest fans, as well as that fan's most popular friends.

Q: What is Twizzlio?
A: A twizzlio is the first trickle of snowmelt, as spring begins after a long and bitter winter. Our app is named after this annual event because we like to view ourselves as a river of active and flowing social data, springing forth from the dull and immobile social data ice of yesterday.
Q: No, I mean, what does Twizzlio actually do?
A: Twizzlio turns your social media connections into a competitive sport. Your social media savvy is tested as you predict which of your own friends, favorite celebrities, and preferred brands will rise in online popularity across Facebook and Twitter. Your choices are matched up against the selections of an opposing player, and your respective teams duke it out in a week-long duel to see who gained the most internet fame in that time.
Q: What do you get if you win?
A: Glory. Bragging rights. Getting to prove that your friends are more important than your opponent's friends, and that your brands are better liked than their brands. You also get badges to prove your victory for all of time.
Q: Hm, sounds like a fun toy.
A: You are very wrong, my friend. Twizzlio is not just a toy. It's also an advertising platform. People and brands can take out ad space next to their name within the game, to deliver messages to every player that uses them in a game (as well as their opponent). This allows you to send exclusive deals to your brand's most loyal followers, those who like your company so much they picked you as their battle champion.
